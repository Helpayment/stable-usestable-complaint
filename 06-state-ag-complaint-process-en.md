# How I Plan to File a State Attorney General Complaint About Stable (www.usestable.com)

> This article explains how I plan to file consumer complaints about Stable / useStable / www.usestable.com. It is not legal advice. A state attorney general usually cannot act as a private lawyer, but consumer complaints can create an official record.

In addition to arbitration, I plan to submit complaints to state consumer protection authorities. Arbitration addresses my dispute with Stable. Consumer complaints help regulators see broader patterns: advertising clarity, billing transparency, account closure fairness, and whether virtual mailbox users are given a reasonable transition period.

Official links:

- California DOJ Consumer Complaint Against A Business/Company: <https://oag.ca.gov/contact/consumer-complaint-against-business-or-company>
- NAAG State Consumer Protection Directory: <https://www.naag.org/our-work/center-for-consumer-protection/consumer-file-a-complaint/>

## Why Complaint Records Matter

A complaint may not immediately recover my losses. But it records that a user experienced problems with Stable / useStable / www.usestable.com.

That record may matter if other users report similar issues:

- passport or document handling problems;
- “unlimited recipients” marketing followed by upgrade pressure;
- sudden Stable account closure;
- refusal to provide a transition period;
- failure to handle mail in transit;
- unclear billing descriptors such as Stable Companions Donations;
- lack of meaningful support response.

## What I Would Include

I would explain that Stable provides virtual business address, remote mailbox, virtual mailbox, mail scanning, and mail forwarding services. I used these Stable services for company operations, so sudden closure created risks far beyond the subscription fee.

I would describe the main issues:

- Stable passport or identity document handling was unclear and, in my view, harmful;
- www.usestable.com pricing or help pages created expectations around “unlimited recipients”;
- after my business address was already tied to institutions, Stable pressured me toward a higher plan;
- the Stable account was closed without a meaningful transition period;
- mail in transit and future mail were not handled with a clear plan;
- Stable billing appeared with a donation-like merchant descriptor.

## Evidence Package

I would prepare:

- a one-page timeline;
- Stable pricing and help center screenshots;
- passport handling communications;
- upgrade and account closure messages;
- my explanations to Stable and Stable’s replies;
- billing screenshots;
- proof that I requested a reasonable resolution.

The public GitHub version will be redacted. A regulator-facing version may include more complete evidence, while still avoiding unnecessary personal information.

## What I Expect

I do not expect a state attorney general complaint to instantly solve everything. But it can create a record, possibly be forwarded to the company, and help authorities see whether similar complaints form a pattern.

For non-U.S. founders, this matters. We cannot easily walk into a U.S. small claims court. We often do not understand the local consumer protection system. Filing complaints is one way to make an otherwise invisible problem visible.
