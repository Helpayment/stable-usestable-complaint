# Why I Am Publishing My Experience With Stable (www.usestable.com)

> This article describes my personal experience with Stable / useStable / www.usestable.com, including Stable virtual address service, Stable virtual mailbox service, mail scanning, mail forwarding, billing records, and the dispute path I am preparing. This is not legal advice.

I used Stable because www.usestable.com offers virtual business address, virtual mailbox, mail scanning, and mail forwarding services. Stable’s Terms of Service identify the contracting party as **Mistro, Inc. dba Stable**.

For a non-U.S. founder operating U.S. companies, a virtual address is not a casual SaaS feature. Once a Stable business address is entered into IRS records, bank accounts, payment platforms, customer profiles, vendor systems, and company documents, it becomes part of the company’s infrastructure.

That is why a sudden rule change, a forced upgrade, an account shutdown, or a refusal to provide a transition period is not just a subscription problem. It can break a company’s ability to receive tax notices, bank letters, customer mail, and government correspondence.

## The Core Problem

Stable virtual address service is not an ordinary subscription product. It is tied to taxes, banks, compliance, customers, vendors, and physical mail. Stable can run risk reviews and can decide not to serve certain users, but when there is no immediate emergency, users should receive a clear explanation, a review path, and a reasonable transition period.

Marketing words like “unlimited,” “included,” and “automatic” should match the actual rules. If a customer buys a Stable virtual mailbox based on www.usestable.com pricing pages or help center language, Stable should not later redefine those words only after the customer’s business is already locked into the address.

Risk review should not become a wall of templates. When a user submits explanations, identity documents, payment history, and business context, the platform should respond to the substance instead of simply closing the account, issuing a refund, and refusing to handle the operational aftermath.

## What I Consider a Reasonable Resolution

I am not asking only for a refund. A refund does not solve identity document damage, address migration, mail in transit, bank notices, IRS letters, or customer communication.

At minimum, I believe Stable / useStable / www.usestable.com should explain:

- why my passport was handled or destroyed earlier than I understood;
- what “unlimited recipients” actually meant on Stable pricing and help pages;
- why my account was closed and what evidence supported that decision;
- why no real transition period was provided;
- whether mail already sent to the old Stable address can be forwarded or recovered;
- why a commercial service payment appeared with a donation-like billing descriptor.

## Why I Am Publishing This

Many non-U.S. founders stay silent when a U.S. technology company or virtual mailbox provider mistreats them. Language, time zones, distance, legal cost, and platform power make the user feel small.

I am publishing this archive to show that users can preserve screenshots, document timelines, request explanations, file arbitration, submit consumer complaints, and create a public record.
